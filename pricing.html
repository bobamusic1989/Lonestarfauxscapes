A complete pricing guide for artificial hedges, faux living walls, and fence extensions in Texas. Updated for 2025.

The cost of artificial hedges varies based on product type, square footage, installation complexity, and fire safety requirements. Here's what Texas property owners and commercial buyers can expect.

What Affects Artificial Hedge Pricing?

Square Footage

Larger projects (500+ sq ft) typically qualify for volume pricing, reducing per-square-foot costs by 10-20%.

Fire Rating

NFPA 701 Method 2 certification is required for most commercial spaces and adds $10-25/sq ft.

Installation Height

Work above 10 feet requires lifts or scaffolding, adding $5-15/sq ft for equipment and labor.

Custom Framing

Complex shapes, corners, or logo cutouts require custom fabrication, typically adding 15-25% to the project.

Acoustic Backing

Sound-dampening backing for noise reduction adds $8-15/sq ft. Popular for pool equipment and alley screens.

Foliage Style

Mixed-tone living walls with multiple plant varieties cost more than single-variety hedge panels.

Residential vs. Commercial Pricing

Residential Projects

Most residential artificial hedge projects in Texas range from $2,500 to $15,000 depending on scope:

  • Fence extension (30 linear ft): $2,500 - $5,000
  • Pool privacy screen (100 sq ft): $5,000 - $9,500
  • Full backyard privacy (200+ sq ft): $10,000 - $20,000

Commercial Projects

Commercial installations typically range from $10,000 to $75,000+ depending on requirements:

  • Restaurant patio screen (150 sq ft): $12,000 - $22,000
  • Hotel lobby feature wall (200 sq ft): $18,000 - $30,000
  • Building facade (500+ sq ft): $45,000 - $75,000+

Commercial buyers: Fire-rated materials, shop drawings, and coordination with general contractors are included in our commercial quotes. We provide documentation for building inspectors and fire marshals.

Why Artificial Hedges Are Cost-Effective

While the upfront cost is higher than planting real hedges, artificial hedges eliminate ongoing expenses:

  • No irrigation: Save $500-2,000/year on water and systems
  • No trimming: Save $300-1,200/year on landscaping
  • No replacement: Real hedges often need replanting every 3-5 years in Texas heat
  • Instant results: No waiting 2-5 years for hedges to mature
  • 5-10 year lifespan: UV-stabilized materials rated for Texas sun

Most property owners see full ROI within 3-4 years compared to maintaining real hedges.
