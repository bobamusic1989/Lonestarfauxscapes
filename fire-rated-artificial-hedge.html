NFPA 701 Method 2 Requirements

To pass NFPA 701 Method 2 testing, materials must:

  • Self-extinguish within 2 seconds
  • Have after-flame time under 2 seconds
  • Limit char length to specified parameters
  • Pass drip-flame test (no flaming drips)

All our fire-rated products meet or exceed these requirements.

Fire-Rated Hedge FAQs

Common questions about fire-rated artificial hedges and code compliance.

What is NFPA 701 Method 2 certification?

NFPA 701 Method 2 is the fire test standard for decorative materials and textiles used in public occupancies. Materials must self-extinguish within 2 seconds and have limited flame propagation. This certification is required for artificial plants in most commercial settings including hotels, restaurants, and multifamily buildings.

Do you provide fire certification documentation?

Yes. We provide official NFPA 701 Method 2 test certificates with every commercial installation. This documentation is required for fire marshal inspections and building code compliance. We can also provide additional documentation as needed for specific permit requirements.

Are fire-rated artificial hedges required in Texas?

Fire-rated materials are required in most commercial occupancies in Texas, including hotels, restaurants, retail stores, multifamily housing common areas, hospitals, and schools. Local fire marshals may require NFPA 701 certification for any artificial decorative materials. Requirements vary by jurisdiction, so we recommend confirming with your local authority having jurisdiction (AHJ).

Do fire-rated hedges look different from regular artificial hedges?

No. Our fire-rated artificial hedges are visually identical to non-rated versions. The fire-retardant treatment is applied during manufacturing and does not affect the appearance, texture, or color of the foliage. You get the same premium aesthetic with the added safety certification.

What if my fire marshal requests additional documentation?

We provide support throughout the inspection process. If your fire marshal requests additional documentation, clarification, or specifications beyond what we initially provide, we can supply supplementary materials. We've worked with fire marshals across Texas and understand their requirements.

Can residential projects use fire-rated materials?

Absolutely. While fire rating isn't typically required for single-family residential, many homeowners in high-fire-risk areas choose fire-rated materials for added safety. The cost difference is minimal and provides extra peace of mind, especially for outdoor installations near structures.
