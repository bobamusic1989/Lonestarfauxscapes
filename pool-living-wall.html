Pool Living Wall FAQs

Common questions about artificial living walls for pool areas.

Can living walls be installed near pools and water features?

Yes. Our artificial living walls are specifically designed to handle pool environments. The materials resist chlorine, humidity, and splash exposure. Unlike real plants that struggle with pool chemicals and reflected heat, artificial living walls thrive in these conditions and look lush year-round.

What styles of living walls work best for pool areas?

Tropical mixed foliage is the most popular choice for pool areas, featuring combinations of ferns, philodendrons, and palm varieties that create a resort-like atmosphere. We also offer modern boxwood styles for a more manicured look, or can mix styles to match your existing landscaping.

Do pool living walls require irrigation or drainage?

No. Artificial living walls require zero irrigation, no drainage systems, and no water supply. This eliminates the complexity and ongoing costs of real living wall systems, which require pumps, timers, fertilizer injection, and constant monitoring. Our walls look just as lush with zero infrastructure.

How do I clean the living wall?

Just rinse with a garden hose a few times a year if dust or pollen accumulates. That's it. No pruning, no fertilizing, no plant replacement. Most homeowners find they rarely need to clean at all since rain handles most dust.

Will the colors fade in Texas sun?

Our materials are UV-stabilized and rated for 5-10 years of direct Texas sun exposure. We use the same commercial-grade materials installed at hotels and resorts. Expect your living wall to maintain its color and appearance for years.

Can you match my existing landscaping style?

Yes. We offer multiple foliage varieties and can custom-mix to complement your existing plants and hardscape. During your consultation, we'll bring samples so you can see how different options look in your actual space and lighting.
